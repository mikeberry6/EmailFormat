Infrastructure Sponsor M&A Activity
Weekly Briefing • February 14–20, 2026
KEY THEMES
Digital infrastructure led this week with four transactions accounting for half the deal count, headlined by the £2B sale of UK wholesale fibre platform Netomnia to nexfibre and MAM’s $952M carve-out of IHS Towers’ Latin American wireless operations. Transport delivered the week’s largest single transaction in MAM’s A$11.7B take-private of Australian logistics operator Qube Holdings, while Blackstone entered Greek airport concessions with a 10% stake in Fraport Greece.
Digital 4 Deals
Substantial Group (Netomnia) Fiber · UK
Acquirer: nexfibre (InfraVia / Liberty Global / Telefónica)
Seller: DigitalBridge
A DigitalBridge fund and its co-shareholders agreed to sell Substantial Group, the parent company of UK wholesale full-fibre operator Netomnia, to nexfibre at a £2B enterprise value. InfraVia, alongside Liberty Global and Telefónica (via their joint venture nexfibre), agreed to acquire the business, with InfraVia directly committing £850M of the £1B in new net equity funding for the transaction.
 
IHS Towers LatAm Operations Towers · Brazil / Colombia
Acquirer: Macquarie Asset Management
Seller: IHS Towers
Macquarie Asset Management agreed to acquire IHS Towers’ Latin American operations (IHS Brazil and IHS Colombia) for an enterprise value of approximately $952M. The carve-out buyout includes over 8,500 wireless tower sites in Brazil and 270 sites in Colombia.
 
Connected Infra Group (Platform) Towers · US
Acquirer: TPG (Peppertree)
Through its TPG Peppertree arm, TPG announced a definitive agreement committing up to $1B in equity to launch Connected Infra Group, a new platform focused on acquiring and operating telecommunications real estate, primarily macro wireless towers in the US.
 
ATC Europe (Minority Stake) Towers · Europe
Acquirer: Commerz Real & AllianzGI
Seller: Allianz Insurance Companies
Commerz Real and Allianz Global Investors (acting as fund manager) acquired a minority equity stake in telecom tower operator ATC Europe. The stake was sold by Allianz insurance companies.
Transportation 2 Deals
Fraport Greece (10% Stake) Airports · Greece
Acquirer: Blackstone (Infrastructure Strategies)
Seller: Marguerite Fund
Blackstone, via its Infrastructure Strategies Fund, acquired a 10% minority equity stake in Fraport Greece from the Marguerite Fund for approximately €200M ($236.2M). Fraport Greece holds a 40-year concession to operate, maintain, and develop 14 regional airports across the country.
 
Qube Holdings Logistics · Australia
Acquirer: MAM-led Consortium
A Macquarie Asset Management-led consortium agreed to a take-private buyout of Qube Holdings Limited for an A$11.7B enterprise value. Qube’s core assets include a supply chain network, rail terminals, bulk logistics facilities, and a 50% stake in Patrick Terminals.
Power & ET 1 Deal
One Power Distributed Generation · US
Acquirer: OnSite Partners (Basalt Infrastructure)
OnSite Partners, wholly owned by Basalt Infrastructure Partners-advised funds, acquired the business interests of Ohio-based industrial power operator One Power, expanding the firm’s distributed energy generation portfolio in the Midwest.
Waste & ES 1 Deal
Kalfresh Bioenergy · Australia
Acquirer: QIC & Wollemi Capital
QIC (Queensland Investment Corporation) and Wollemi Capital made an A$80M equity climate investment into Kalfresh. The funds will be used to construct a 40-hectare integrated food and energy precinct featuring an anaerobic digestion bioenergy facility.

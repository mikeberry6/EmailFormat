Key Infrastructure M&A Trends
 
The infrastructure M&A landscape continues to accelerate into Q1 2026, driven by convergent demand across digital infrastructure, energy transition, and water systems. Strategic acquirers and financial sponsors are deploying capital at unprecedented scale, underscoring the sector’s position at the nexus of secular growth themes.
Trend 01
Data Center Build-Outs Fueled by AI Demand
Hyperscaler capex commitments for AI-ready infrastructure have surpassed $180B globally, catalyzing a wave of M&A in the data center and digital infrastructure value chain. Targets with shovel-ready land positions, secured power capacity, and fiber connectivity are commanding premium multiples. We see continued consolidation among colocation providers and edge computing platforms as operators seek geographic and capacity diversification.
Trend 02
Energy Resiliency & the Strategic Super Cycle
Grid reliability concerns and electrification of industrial processes are driving a “Strategic Super Cycle” in energy infrastructure. Gas-fired generation assets have re-emerged as critical baseload resources, with transaction volumes in the natural gas power segment up 40% year-over-year. Simultaneously, renewable energy platforms with integrated storage solutions continue to attract significant sponsor interest as utilities accelerate decarbonization timelines amid rising power demand.
Trend 03
The Rise of Mega-Deals (>$5B)
Infrastructure has entered a new era of deal scale. Transactions exceeding $5B in enterprise value accounted for over 35% of total sector volume in the trailing twelve months—a record share. Large-cap infrastructure funds, sovereign wealth vehicles, and strategic corporates are the primary drivers, reflecting conviction in the long-duration, inflation-linked return profile of core infrastructure assets. Platform acquisitions with bolt-on optionality remain the preferred execution strategy.

Sector Focus
US Water Infrastructure
The US water infrastructure TAM is projected to exceed $1.1T over the next decade, driven by aging municipal systems, PFAS remediation mandates, and increasing demand from industrial end-markets. Private capital penetration remains below 15%, presenting a significant runway for consolidation. We expect M&A activity to intensify as regulated water utilities pursue acquisition-led growth strategies and environmental compliance timelines compress under the latest EPA directives.
Sector Focus
Gas-Fired Power Generation
Natural gas generation capacity is experiencing a renaissance as the critical bridge fuel for AI-driven load growth. With data center power demand forecast to add 35–50 GW of incremental load by 2030, gas-fired assets with firm capacity contracts and proximity to hyperscale campuses are trading at 8–10x EBITDA—a significant premium to historical averages. Behind-the-meter gas generation for data centers represents an emerging M&A sub-theme, with several platform builds currently in execution.

Sector Focus
Environmental Services
Environmental services M&A remains robust, propelled by tightening regulatory frameworks around PFAS/PFOS contamination, hazardous waste disposal, and industrial remediation. The US environmental services market, valued at approximately $430B, continues to consolidate around scaled platforms with national permitting capabilities. Private equity sponsors are actively pursuing roll-up strategies in specialty waste management, soil remediation, and environmental consulting—segments where fragmentation creates meaningful arbitrage between entry and platform-level multiples.

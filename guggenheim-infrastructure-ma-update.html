Global Infrastructure M&A Activity

Key Themes
Deal flow this week was headlined by Blackstone & EQT’s $6.6B joint acquisition of Urbaser, one of the largest environmental services transactions of 2026. Power & energy transition accounted for half the week’s activity, with CPP Investments’ $3.4B entry into Inkia Energy marking continued sovereign capital deployment into Latin American generation platforms. Digital infrastructure consolidation continued with InfraBridge’s fiber merger in the UK.

Deal of the Week
         
Urbaser Spain
Platinum Equity → Blackstone & EQT 		$6.6B
Joint acquisition of Urbaser, a Madrid-headquartered global waste management platform serving 60M+ people across 15 countries with 50,000+ employees, from Platinum Equity at ~$6.6B (€5.6B). Each buyer will hold a 50% stake. Under Platinum’s ownership since 2021, Urbaser grew revenue by 60% and EBITDA by 70%; Platinum retains the Argentina business (~$90M EBITDA) as a carve-out.

Inkia Energy (50% Stake) Peru
I Squared Capital → CPP Investments 		CPP Investments to acquire 50% of Inkia Energy, a Peruvian power generation platform operating a diversified 2.6 GW portfolio with a 4+ GW development pipeline of wind, solar, gas, and battery storage, at an enterprise value of ~$3.4B. Centrica Energy Solutions Italy / Netherlands
Centrica → 3i Infrastructure (Joulz) 		3i portfolio company Joulz completed acquisition of Centrica’s non-core energy solutions businesses in Italy and the Netherlands, offering C&I customers onsite CHP, solar, battery storage, and energy management. Part of a broader Centrica disposal exceeding £80M as it recycles capital to focus on core growth priorities.

Fig Power (1.7 GW Pipeline) UK
Acquired by InfraRed (TRIG) 		TRIG acquired 100% of Fig Power, a Bristol-based battery energy storage developer with a 1.7 GW UK pipeline comprising nine advanced projects and exclusive sites. TRIG committed ~£20M over two years, gaining proprietary development capabilities in storage to complement its existing renewable generation portfolio.

CleanMax (6.79% Stake) India
Pre-IPO investment by Temasek 		Temasek acquired a 6.79% stake in CleanMax, India’s largest C&I renewable energy provider operating 2.54 GW of solar capacity serving 500+ customers including Apple, Google, and Amazon, as part of an ₹1,185 crore (~$88M) pre-IPO round. Investment came days before CleanMax’s ₹5,200 crore IPO opening February 20.

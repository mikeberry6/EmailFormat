Weekly Briefing – Infrastructure Sponsor M&A – 8 deals totaling $10.1B+ across Power, Midstream, Transportation, Digital, and Environmental Services – Feb 7–13, 2026
GUGGENHEIM
INFRASTRUCTURE COVERAGE & ADVISORY
Infrastructure Sponsor M&A
Weekly Briefing • February 7–13, 2026
KEY THEMES
Deal flow this week was headlined by Blackstone & EQT’s $6.6B joint acquisition of Urbaser, one of the largest environmental services transactions in recent years. Power & energy transition accounted for half the week’s activity, with CPP Investments’ $3.4B entry into Inkia Energy marking continued sovereign capital deployment into Latin American generation platforms. Digital infrastructure consolidation continued with InfraBridge’s fiber merger in the UK.
Power & ET 4 Deals
Inkia Energy (50% Stake) Generation · Peru
Acquirer: CPP Investments
Seller: I Squared Capital
CPP Investments to acquire 50% of Inkia Energy, a Peruvian power generation platform operating a diversified 2.6 GW portfolio with a 4+ GW development pipeline of wind, solar, gas, and battery storage, at an enterprise value of ~$3.4B. I Squared Capital, invested since 2017, rolled its remaining stake into a continuation vehicle.
Source
 
Centrica Energy Solutions Energy Services · Italy / Netherlands
Acquirer: 3i Infrastructure (Joulz)
Seller: Centrica
3i portfolio company Joulz completed acquisition of Centrica’s non-core energy solutions businesses in Italy and the Netherlands, offering C&I customers onsite CHP, solar, battery storage, and energy management. Part of a broader Centrica disposal exceeding £80M+ as it recycles capital to focus on core growth priorities.
Source
 
Fig Power (1.7 GW Pipeline) Storage · UK
Acquirer: InfraRed (TRIG)
TRIG acquired 100% of Fig Power, a Bristol-based battery energy storage developer with a 1.7 GW UK pipeline comprising nine advanced projects and exclusive sites. TRIG committed ~£20M over two years, gaining proprietary development capabilities in storage to complement its existing renewable generation portfolio.
Source
 
CleanMax (6.79% Stake) Solar · India
Acquirer: Temasek (Pre-IPO)
Temasek acquired a 6.79% stake in CleanMax, India’s largest C&I renewable energy provider operating 2.54 GW of solar capacity serving 500+ customers including Apple, Google, and Amazon, as part of an ₹1,185 crore (~$88M) pre-IPO round. Investment came days before CleanMax’s ₹5,200 crore IPO opening February 20.
Source
Digital 1 Deal
Truespeed & Freedom Fibre Fiber · UK
Merger: InfraBridge & Equitix
Freedom Fibre and Truespeed agreed to merge, creating a scaled full-fibre broadband platform with 412K premises across North West, West Midlands, South West, and East of England with ~70K customers. Combines Truespeed’s retail brand with Freedom Fibre’s open-access wholesale model, positioning the entity as a consolidation leader in the UK altnet sector.
Source
Midstream 1 Deal
Delaware Basin Residue (90%) Gas Pipeline · US
Acquirer: I Squared Capital
Seller: WhiteWater Midstream
I Squared acquired a 90% stake in Delaware Basin Residue from WhiteWater Midstream, an intrastate natural gas pipeline spanning 200+ miles with 3+ Bcf/d capacity connecting Delaware Basin processing plants to the Waha Hub. Feeds into major downstream connections including the Whistler Pipeline to Gulf Coast LNG export corridors.
Source
Transportation 1 Deal
American Roads Toll Roads · US
Acquirer: John Laing (KKR)
Seller: CVC DIF
John Laing (KKR) agreed to acquire American Roads from CVC DIF, a portfolio of four tolled facilities including three perpetual-right toll bridges in Alabama and the US-side concession of the Detroit-Windsor Tunnel, a strategic international border crossing serving ~7M trips annually.
Source
Waste & ES 1 Deal
Urbaser Waste Mgmt · Spain
Acquirer: Blackstone & EQT
Seller: Platinum Equity
Joint acquisition of Urbaser, a Madrid-headquartered global waste management platform serving 60M+ people across 15 countries with 50,000+ employees, from Platinum Equity at ~$6.6B (€5.6B). Each buyer will hold a 50% stake. Under Platinum’s ownership since 2021, Urbaser grew revenue by 60% and EBITDA by 70%; Platinum retains the Argentina business (~$90M EBITDA) as a carve-out.
